## Cross-Cutting Issues

### 1. No Privacy Policy or Terms of Service Page
No `/privacy-policy` or `/terms` route found in `/src/app/`. Contact forms collect personal/sensitive information from people in vulnerable situations (infidelity, custody, financial investigations). YMYL pages with lead capture forms and no privacy policy fail Google's trustworthiness criteria outright and likely violate California CCPA.

**Severity: Critical.**

### 2. No Physical Street Address
Footer lists "Southern California." Background-checks copy mentions "Murrieta, CA" and "San Diego, CA" casually in body text — but no consistent business address is displayed. For a YMYL business, Google expects verifiable NAP (Name, Address, Phone). CALI and World Assoc of Detectives memberships could be verified by address.

**Severity: High.**

### 3. Inconsistent Phone Numbers
- Main number: 909-964-8976
- Asset search callout (/background-checks): 909-220-4006
- Consulting CTA footer: 909-602-1930

Three different numbers across the site. Destroys NAP consistency for local SEO and creates trust confusion for users.

**Severity: High.**

### 4. Experience Years Inconsistency
- Everywhere: "27+ years of experience"
- /risk-management whyChoose array: "Over 15 years experience"
- Stats section: "20+ Years Experience"

Three different numbers on the same site. Any user or QR who cross-checks will flag this.

**Severity: High.**

### 5. Duplicate / Boilerplate FAQ Answers
On /consulting, three FAQ questions about civil consulting, trial consulting, and jury consulting all have identical answers ("We have worked on cases with attorneys all across Southern California and have formed business relationships due to our reliability and close rate."). The "What are the benefits of choosing GA Tucker PI?" FAQ reappears verbatim on background-checks and consulting.

**Severity: Medium.** Signals templated/boilerplate content to Google.

### 6. Primary Contact Email is Gmail
`gatuckerpi@gmail.com` is displayed as the business email on every service page, sidebar, and footer. For a YMYL professional service charging premium rates, a domain email (e.g., greg@gatuckerpi.com) is a basic trustworthiness signal. Gmail suggests a low-investment operation to skeptical users and QRs.

**Severity: Medium.**

### 7. No Author Bylines on Service Pages
None of the service pages carry a byline attributing the content to Greg Tucker. Google's Sept 2025 QRG explicitly looks for authorship signals on YMYL content. A brief "Written by Greg A. Tucker, CA Licensed PI #188351" with a link to /about on each service page would provide a strong authorship chain.

**Severity: Medium.**

### 8. "Read More Reviews" Link Goes Nowhere
On /background-checks the "Read More Reviews" link points to `href="#"` — a dead anchor. If this is meant to link to Google Reviews or Yelp, it must be connected. Empty links undermine trustworthiness.

**Severity: Medium.**

### 9. Julia's Credentials Undertapped
Julia Tucker — 17 years as a psychologist, master's degrees in education and child psychology, paralegal certification — is mentioned only on /about and not referenced on /infidelity, /child-custody, or /missing-persons where her background is directly material to client decisions. Her psychology credential is a genuine E-E-A-T differentiator that almost no PI firm can match.

**Severity: Medium** (missed opportunity, not a defect).

### 10. No BBB or Google Review Integration
No BBB accreditation badge, no embedded Google Review widget, no Yelp rating display. The Yelp link in the footer goes to the correct listing (Murrieta), which is good — but no review count or rating is surfaced on the site.

**Severity: Medium.**
